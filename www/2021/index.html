Was it hot in 2021?

The last time we did a year in review was 2019, in the middle of Australia's catastrophic summer of bushfires and a year of crippling drought. By contrast, 2021 was a year of La Nina, meaning cooler temperatures and greater rainfall on Australia's east coast.

Overall, Darwin had a very hot year (ranked #2 since 1910) as did Perth (ranked #9 since 1910), while Sydney West (Richmond) had a cool year (ranked #51 since 1941).

You may have also noticed some of our stations stopped working in the past few years. This was because some of our analysis broke when the Bureau of Meterology moved stations and associated station IDs, including Sydney, Melbourne and Adelaide stations. In 2022, with a recent update of the underlying historical data (ACORN-SAT), we'll be able to restore these stations.
